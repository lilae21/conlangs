Knowledge bases

  • The World Atlas of Language Structures (WALS) Online, a large database of structural (phonological, grammatical, lexical) properties of languages gathered from descriptive materials (such as reference grammars) by a team of 55 authors.
  • CLICS3, the Database of Cross-Linguistic Colexifications, an online database of colexifications in over 3150 languages of the world.
  • The IndieWeb, "a people-focused alternative to the 'corporate web'".

Articles, documents, references, sources

  • A Conlanger's Thesaurus by William S. Annis, on Fiat Lingua
  • Lingweenie's (William S. Annis) article on Number of Conlang Root Words
  • The (searchable) Index Diachronica
  • Mark Rosenfelder's conlanging and conworlding work probably does not need to be introduced to conlangers, but his articles and book reviews are no less interesting: an extremely interesting one about conworlding is his article on cities, reviewing Jane Jacobs' work.
